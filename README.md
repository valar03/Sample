Findings and ComparisonsPredictive Accuracy: GBM outperforms both fractional regression and decision trees in terms of out-of-sample mean squared error, highlighting its effectiveness in predicting recovery rates.Model Interpretability: Decision trees provide insights into important variables but can generate complex, less interpretable models compared to the more interpretable fractional regression.Variable Importance: Contrasts between models reveal different insights into determinants of recovery rates, particularly highlighting the importance of debt seniority and type of collateral in GBM versus industry sectors in fractional regression.Explaining Black-Box ModelsShapley Values: Used to explain individual predictions by assessing the marginal contribution of each regressor in GBM.Accumulated Local Effects (ALE) Plots: Visualize the relationship between recovery rates and regressors, capturing nonlinear and interaction effects that traditional methods may miss.Practical ImplicationsModel Transparency vs. Accuracy: Demonstrates that high predictive accuracy (GBM) can coexist with model transparency using explainable machine learning techniques.Regulatory Compliance: Suggests that banks can utilize GBM's insights into recovery determinants for better credit risk management aligned with Basel agreements.ConclusionRecommendations: Advocates for the adoption of GBM and other black-box models in credit risk modeling due to their superior predictive performance and ability to capture nuanced relationships.Challenges: Acknowledges computational challenges in interpreting black-box models but justifies the effort based on substantial predictive gains.
